# Ekho Voice Samples

Reference voice for any agent-generated outbound. Source: Rowan's essays, Ekho marketing copy, customer testimonials. Treat as a calibration set, not phrases to copy.

## House voice anchors

- Operator-to-operator. Never marketer-to-customer.
- Concrete over abstract. Numbers, names, states, brands. No "your dealership is unique."
- Anti-Carvana stance is implicit, not preached. The frame is "preserve dealer participation."
- Founder voice is columnist-cadence, with one sharp observation per paragraph.
- Never apologizes. Never hedges. "The answer is never 'AI can't do this'."

## Rowan extracts

> "The 800-pound gorilla just got a franchise license. Stellantis sold Carvana the right to compete with your dealership inside the OEM tent. The dealers who keep selling at full participation in 2027 will be the ones who shipped infrastructure in 2026."

> "Wall-poster strategy is the move where a dealer principal stares at a wall of trim levels and tries to figure out which inventory mix to commit floor-plan to next quarter. AI doesn't fix the wall poster. AI removes the wall."

> "Most dealer outbound today is a BDR with a CRM tab open and a Salesforce loomis-bot keyboard macro. The version that wins is the one where the agent already knows the dealer's brand mix, their nearest Ekho-active competitor, and what to say. We're building that version."

## Subject-line vocabulary that works

- "Your nearest Ekho-active store"
- "Inventory mix question"
- "[Brand] specifically"
- "Worth a fifteen-minute call"
- "Saw your [specific signal]"

## Subject-line vocabulary that's banned

- "Quick question?"
- "Following up"
- "Touching base"
- "Just checking in"
- "Hope this finds you well"
- Anything with an emoji
- Anything ending in "!"

## Email body discipline

- Open with one specific observation about the dealer's site, inventory, or market position. Not a compliment.
- Middle paragraph names a real Ekho-active competitor (illustrative footprint) and what they're using Ekho for. Specific product.
- Close with a single ask. One CTA. No "or let me know what's better for you."
- Length: 80-140 words per email, max. Anything longer signals BDR.
- No company boilerplate at the bottom. Just the byline.
